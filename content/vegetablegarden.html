The initial vegetable garden consisted of two sad looking tomato plants and some poor sandy soil typical of our area. At the time I didn’t know anything about plants or soils or how the whole process came together, but the idea of actually having a productive vegetable garden grew on me and I quickly set to work clearing some land of weeds and putting together some rudimentary garden beds with some old bricks my mum had laying around.

When we first purchased some seedlings from our local garden store the prospect of caring for them all seemed like a daunting prospect. My realisation after setting them down that I had no idea what I was doing with my first thought being “How do I water a plant?” This was going to be a challenge. Fortunately, there are a huge amount of resources out there and a few dozen articles later and hundreds of YouTube videos I was successfully growing all manner of vegetables.

I upgraded the brick veggie patch into four raised beds and moved it into a part of the garden that received more sunlight which is important for a productive garden. The beds were filled using a form of hügelkultur which involves placing sticks and logs on the bottom and stacking them up and finishing with some soil on top. The result is a rich well-draining soil filled with organic matter which is critical for healthy plant growth.
